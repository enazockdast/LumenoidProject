# Cell Sorting

## Scientific Objective

Determine how the magnitude, timing, and spatial regulation of cell-generated
forces control the formation, maintenance, and reversibility of multicellular
patterns.

The project focuses on lumenoids as a tractable three-dimensional epithelial
system. Experiments and models will distinguish four coupled processes:

- cell-cell adhesion;
- actomyosin contractility;
- cell rearrangement and tissue fluidity;
- signaling-dependent assignment of mechanical properties.

## Organizing Framework

Cell sorting is treated as a dynamical self-organization process rather than
only as relaxation toward a minimum-energy configuration:

```text
cell state
    -> adhesion, contractility, and motility
    -> cell rearrangement
    -> new neighbors
    -> updated signaling and cell state
```

The immediate experimental system uses global doxycycline (DOX) induction to
change N-cadherin expression in one cell population. This provides controlled,
time-dependent mechanical asymmetry without neighbor-dependent fate switching.
The longer-term goal is to replace global induction with local cell-cell
signaling so that signaling, fate, and mechanics form a closed feedback loop.

## Near-Term Scientific Opportunity

The first question is whether established sorted domains persist after the
induced N-cadherin difference is removed. Persistence would reveal mechanical
memory, kinetic trapping, low tissue fluidity, or additional stable differences
between the populations. Remixing kinetics would provide a direct measurement
of the rate at which the tissue loses an imposed organization.

In parallel, inducible perturbations of actomyosin activity will test whether
differential contractility can independently generate or modify sorting.
Candidate perturbations include `ROCK1`, `MYH9`, and `MYH10`.
