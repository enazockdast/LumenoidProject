# Lumenoid Growth

## Scientific Question

What physical mechanisms govern the growth, lumen expansion, and epithelial thickening of hiPSC-derived lumenoids?

## Motivation

Many lumenized tissues and organoids exhibit lumen expansion accompanied by thinning of the epithelial shell due to hydrostatic pressure. In contrast, hiPSC lumenoids display progressive thickening of the epithelial layer during growth.

Understanding the origin of this behavior requires integrating cell growth, osmotic transport, epithelial mechanics, and basement membrane mechanics into a unified framework.

## Central Hypothesis

Lumenoid growth is controlled by the interplay between:

1. Cell growth and proliferation
2. Osmotically driven lumen expansion
3. Mechanical relaxation of the epithelial shell

When cell growth proceeds faster than osmotic and elastic relaxation processes, compressive stresses develop within the epithelial shell. Because cells are approximately incompressible, area compression generates increased epithelial thickness.

## Approach

### Experiments

* Time-lapse measurements of inner and outer lumenoid radii
* Single-cell and tissue-scale morphometrics
* Rapamycin perturbations (reduced cell growth)
* TJP1 (ZO-1) CRISPRi perturbations (increased ion permeability)
* Additional perturbations targeting mechanics and transport

### Modeling

Development of a minimal continuum model incorporating:

* Cell growth
* Active ion pumping
* Ion leakage
* Osmotic water transport
* Epithelial shell mechanics
* Basement membrane mechanics

## Key Results

* Model quantitatively reproduces control growth dynamics.
* Reduced growth under rapamycin predicts reduced epithelial thickening.
* ZO-1 perturbations strongly support a strong-osmotic-flow regime.
* A single mechanistic framework explains control and perturbation datasets.

## Long-Term Goal

Develop predictive and interpretable models linking cellular growth, tissue mechanics, and transport processes in lumenized multicellular systems.
