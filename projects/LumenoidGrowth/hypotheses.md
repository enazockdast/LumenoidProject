# Hypotheses

## H1: Cell Growth Generates Epithelial Thickening

Statement:

When cell growth occurs faster than osmotic and elastic relaxation processes, compressive stresses accumulate within the epithelial shell, causing epithelial thickening.

Prediction:

Thickness increases with increasing growth rate.

Evidence:

* Control experiments show progressive thickening.
* Rapamycin reduces growth rate and reduces thickening.
* Continuum model reproduces observed behavior.

Confidence:

High

---

## H2: Lumenoid Growth Operates in a Strong Osmotic Flow Regime

Statement:

Lumen growth is primarily controlled by osmotic transport rather than growth-limited expansion.

Prediction:

Increasing ion permeability should strongly reduce lumen size and enhance shell thickening.

Evidence:

* TJP1 (ZO-1) perturbations cause shrinking lumen radius.
* Strong agreement with model predictions in strong-flow regime.

Confidence:

High

---

## H3: Cell Incompressibility Couples Area Compression to Thickness Growth

Statement:

Cell volume is approximately conserved, so shell area compression directly generates increased epithelial thickness.

Prediction:

Single-cell volume remains approximately constant during thickening.

Evidence:

* Mechanistic basis of current model.
* Requires further validation using single-cell measurements.

Confidence:

Medium

---

## H4: Growth, Osmotic Transport, and Mechanics Form a Minimal Predictive Framework

Statement:

The coupled dynamics of cell growth, ion transport, osmotic flows, and shell mechanics are sufficient to explain observed lumenoid dynamics.

Prediction:

A single parameterized model should explain multiple perturbation datasets without re-fitting unrelated parameters.

Evidence:

* Control dataset.
* Rapamycin perturbations.
* TJP1 perturbations.

Confidence:

High
